Bloomington, Indiana

Local resources

A curated list of places we love around town — whether you are new to Bloomington, building a business here, or just want to get to know the city better. We update this page as we discover more.

New in town

Start here if you have just arrived or are planning a visit.

  • Visit Bloomington

    Visit site →

    The official visitor guide — events calendar, neighborhood overviews, and seasonal highlights across Monroe County.

  • Kirkwood Avenue

    View map →

    Downtown's main strip — restaurants, shops, street life, and the feel of the city in one walkable corridor.

  • Monroe County History Center

    Visit site →

    Local history exhibits and programs — a quick way to understand the place beyond the university.

  • Bloomingfoods Co-op

    Visit site →

    Community-owned grocery with multiple locations — good for stocking a kitchen and meeting neighbors who care about local food.

Eat & drink

Where we send clients for a good meal and where we grab coffee between meetings.

  • Soma Coffeehouse

    Visit site →

    Downtown cafe with reliable Wi-Fi and a steady hum of regulars — a practical spot for informal meetings or solo work.

  • Runcible Spoon

    Visit site →

    A Bloomington institution — coffee, breakfast, and a laid-back atmosphere that has anchored the near-east side for decades.

  • Upland Brewing Co.

    Visit site →

    One of the city's best-known local brands — tasting room, seasonal beers, and a case study in hometown identity done right.

  • Farmers' Market at Showers Common

    Visit site →

    Saturday mornings downtown — produce, bakers, makers, and the best pulse on what local small businesses are up to.

Work & create

Spaces and stops for creative professionals and anyone building something here.

  • Monroe County Public Library

    Visit site →

    Downtown main branch — quiet tables, free resources, and a public space that welcomes long afternoons of focused work.

  • The Mill

    Visit site →

    Coworking, events, and a startup community under one roof — useful if you are meeting founders or looking for a professional network in town.

  • John Waldron Arts Center

    Visit site →

    Gallery exhibitions and performances near the courthouse square — a good window into the local arts scene.

  • Fountain Square Mall

    Visit site →

    Independent shops and studios inside a historic downtown building — worth a slow walk when you are looking for locally owned retail.

Outdoors

Where to clear your head after a long project week.

  • Griffy Lake Nature Preserve

    Visit site →

    Woodland trails and a reservoir minutes from downtown — the default answer when someone asks where to hike close to town.

  • Bryan Park

    Visit site →

    Tennis, playgrounds, and open green space on the east side — easy to reach and good for families settling into the neighborhood.

  • Lake Monroe & Paynetown

    Visit site →

    The big lake south of town — boating, shoreline trails, and a wider sense of southern Indiana within a short drive.

Arts & culture

Performance, film, and the creative energy that shapes Bloomington's character.

  • Buskirk-Chumley Theater

    Visit site →

    Live music, film, comedy, and community events in a restored downtown theater — check the calendar before any weekend in town.

  • Lotus Education & Arts Foundation

    Visit site →

    World music and arts programming year-round — the fall festival is a city-wide gathering worth planning around.

  • Indiana University Campus

    Visit site →

    Museums, architecture, lectures, and Division I athletics — the university is the cultural engine of the city even if you are not enrolled.
